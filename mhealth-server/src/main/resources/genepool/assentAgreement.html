Assent

Approval Date: January 9, 2017

1. What will happen to me in this study?

You will be asked to provide a blood sample and to agree to give the research team access to your medical records. In some cases Stanford is collaborating with outside institutions and your samples may be sent to these institutions for testing.

2. Can anything bad happen to me?

There are minimal risks associated with drawing blood, these include bleeding underneath the skin, bruising, scarring and repeated punctures to locate a vein. If you start feeling sick or are in pain because of the blood draw please tell your parent(s).

3. Can anything good happen to me?

There will be no benefits to you for participating in this study but you may be contributing to helping us find out more about a number of different health problems, this knowledge could help other kids in the future.

4. Do I have other choices?

You can decline to participate in this study.

5. Will anyone know I am in the study?

Only the medical doctor that is in charge of the study and his research team will know that you are in the study.

6. What happens if I get hurt?

The risks in this study are very minimal but if you do get hurt because of your participation in the study there will be trained medical doctors on site to help you.

7. Who can I talk to about the study?

If you have any questions about the study or any problems to do with the study you can contact the Protocol Director (Thomas Quertermous). You can call him at (650)-723-5013. You can also call or email Casandra Trowbridge, Project Manager for the Metabolic Health Center, ctbridge@stanford.edu, (650)-736-7435. If you have questions about the study but want to talk to someone else who is not a part of the study, you can call the Stanford Institutional Review Board (IRB) at (650)-723-5244 or toll free at 1-866-680-2906.

8. What if I do not want to do this?

You are in no way obligated to participate, you can say no to the study.

9. What if I want to provide a urine sample as well?

If you want to providea urine sample as well as the required blood sample, please indicate that information on the consent form. The lab personnel will be notified, and you can submit both blood and urine samples during the same visit.
